Sunflower-Basic Information

Sunflowers (*Helianthus annuus*) are annual herbaceous plants in the Asteraceae family, native to North and Central America. They are known for their tall, sturdy stems (up to 3 meters) and large, bright yellow flower heads that follow the sun, a phenomenon called heliotropism. The central disk contains numerous small florets that develop into edible seeds, rich in oil and nutrients. Beyond their striking appearance, sunflowers carry deep symbolic meanings. They are often associated with adoration, loyalty, and longevity due to their sun-seeking nature. In many cultures, sunflowers represent positivity, warmth, and hope, making them a popular choice for gifts and decorations.

Guardians of Time: Gentle Care Guide

Water Management

Sunflowers prefer moderate watering.When placed in a vase, ensure the water covers about one-third of the stem and change the water every 2-3 days to prevent bacterial growth.

Temperature And Light

Sunflowers thrive in bright, direct sunlight. Place them in a sunny location and maintain a warm environment to encourage healthy growth.

Stem Pruning

Trim the sunflower stems at an angle every 2-3 days when kept in a vase. This helps improve water absorption and extends the flower’s lifespan. Remove any leaves submerged in water to prevent rot.

Flower Care

Keep sunflowers away from strong winds and heat sources. To maintain their freshness, mist the petals lightly and ensure good air circulation around them.
