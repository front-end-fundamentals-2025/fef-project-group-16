Jasmine-Basic Information

Jasmine (*Jasminum*) is a genus of evergreen or deciduous shrubs and vines in the Oleaceae family, widely distributed in tropical and subtropical regions such as South Asia, China, and the Mediterranean. These plants are well known for their small, star-shaped flowers, usually white or yellow, which emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially in the evening. Jasmine has significant cultural and symbolic value. In many traditions, it represents purity, love, and spiritual beauty. It is commonly used in perfumes, teas, and religious ceremonies. The delicate yet resilient nature of jasmine makes it a symbol of grace and elegance in different cultures.

Guardians of Time: Gentle Care Guide

Water Management

Jasmine plants prefer slightly moist but well-drained soil. Water them regularly. If placed in a vase, change the water every 2-3 days and ensure only the lower stem is submerged.

Temperature And Light

Jasmine thrives in bright, indirect sunlight and warm temperatures. Avoid exposing it to harsh direct sunlight for prolonged periods, as excessive heat can dry out the flowers.

Stem Pruning

Trim the jasmine stems slightly every few days when kept in a vase. This helps maintain water absorption and prevents wilting. Remove any spent flowers to encourage prolonged blooming.

Flower Care

Mist jasmine flowers regularly to maintain humidity and keep their fragrance fresh. Keep them away from strong drafts and excessive dryness to prevent premature wilting.
